Spotting Grant/Scholarship Scams

Plain-language rules:

  • No fees. Legit scholarships/grants don’t charge application or “processing” fees.
  • No guarantees. “Guaranteed approval” or “everyone qualifies” = red flag.
  • Verify the sender & site. Prefer official domains (e.g., .gov, schools, known nonprofits). Be cautious with look-alike URLs.
  • Protect your SSN & bank info. Don’t share sensitive data just to “check eligibility.”
  • Beware urgency & pressure. “Act now” countdowns are classic scam tactics.

Student Checklist

  • I confirmed the website is an official domain (school, trusted nonprofit, or .gov).
  • No application or “processing” fees requested.
  • No SSN/bank info required just to apply.
  • There’s a real contact (school office or org with phone/address).
  • I read the eligibility/terms (award amount, renewal rules, deadlines).

Family Checklist

  • Source is verified (state agency, school district, known nonprofit, or .gov).
  • No upfront fees, gift-card requests, or wire transfers.
  • Requested documents make sense (income proof for benefits, transcripts for scholarships).
  • Email looks legit (no typos, matches the org domain, links preview to the same site).
  • I saved copies of the listing, application, and confirmations.

If something feels off: stop and verify with a counselor, school aid office, or the program’s official phone line. You can also search the program name + “scam” and check government consumer alerts.
